üõèÔ∏è Bed Placement for Children

Where your child sleeps dramatically affects their rest quality, health, and emotional stability:

The Command Position

  • See the door ‚Äî Child should be able to see who enters (sense of security)
  • Not in line with door ‚Äî Direct door alignment creates rushing chi
  • Solid wall behind ‚Äî Headboard against a solid wall provides support
  • Head pointing East ‚Äî East direction supports growth and new beginnings

‚úÖ Good Bed Placement

  • Diagonal from door, can see entrance
  • Solid headboard against wall
  • Space on both sides of bed
  • Away from windows and radiators
  • Not under sloped ceiling or beams

‚ùå Bad Bed Placement

  • Directly in line with door
  • Head under window
  • Under exposed beams or slopes
  • Facing bathroom door or stairs
  • Sharing wall with bathroom/kitchen

‚ö†Ô∏è Bunk Bed Concerns

Bunk beds are not ideal in feng shui. The upper child has no ceiling support (instability), while the lower child has pressure from above (stress). If you must use bunk beds, place a canopy or fabric above the lower bunk to create a symbolic "ceiling."

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best feng shui direction for a child's bed?
Children should sleep with their head pointing toward their personal Kua direction or East (for growth energy). The bed should be in the command position ‚Äî seeing the door but not directly in line with it. Avoid beds under windows or beams.
Where should a study desk be placed in a child's room?
Place the study desk in the Northeast corner (knowledge sector) of the room. The child should face a solid wall (not a window) while studying to maintain focus. Ensure good lighting from the left side for right-handed children.
What colors are best for a child's feng shui bedroom?
Soft, muted colors work best: light blue for calming energy, soft green for growth, pale yellow for cheerfulness, or gentle pink for nurturing. Avoid bright red, black, or overly stimulating colors that can cause hyperactivity or poor sleep.
Can children have mirrors in their bedroom?
Mirrors should not face the bed as they can cause restless sleep and nightmares in children. Small mirrors for dressing are fine but should be covered at night or positioned away from the sleeping area.
