üè† Room-by-Room Color Guide

Living Room

The living room is the heart of your home where family gathers and guests are entertained. Colors here should promote harmony and positive social energy.

Best Colors:

  • Earth tones (beige, tan, terracotta) - Grounding and welcoming
  • Warm whites - Open and clean energy
  • Soft greens - Growth and harmony
  • Gold/Yellow accents - Prosperity and joy

Avoid: Too much red (overstimulating), all white (cold), all black (heavy)

Bedroom

The bedroom should promote rest, romance, and rejuvenation. Colors here should be calming yet supportive of love and relationships.

Best Colors:

  • Soft pinks - Romance and love
  • Skin tones (peach, coral, cream) - Sensuality and comfort
  • Earth tones - Grounding for restful sleep
  • Soft lavender - Calming and spiritual

Avoid: Bright red (too stimulating), blue (too cold for romance), green (growth energy disrupts sleep)

üí° Bedroom Tip: If you're single and want to attract love, add pink or red accents in pairs (two pillows, two candles). Read our Bedroom Feng Shui Guide for more tips!

Kitchen

The kitchen represents wealth and nourishment. Colors should support the fire element (cooking) while maintaining cleanliness and appetite.

Best Colors:

  • White - Clean and fresh
  • Earth tones - Nourishment and stability
  • Yellow - Happiness and wealth
  • Green accents - Freshness and health

Avoid: Red (fire conflict with stove), blue/black (water puts out fire)

Home Office

Your workspace should promote focus, creativity, and career success.

Best Colors:

  • Green - Growth and new ideas
  • Blue-green (teal) - Combines career flow with growth
  • White/cream - Clarity and focus
  • Gold accents - Prosperity and recognition

Avoid: Bright red (anxious energy), too much gray (depressing), heavy black

Bathroom

Bathrooms have draining water energy, so colors here should counteract that effect.

Best Colors:

  • Earth tones - Controls water energy
  • White - Clean and fresh
  • Green accents - Wood weakens water
  • Warm neutrals - Comfortable and spa-like

Avoid: Blue and black (adds more water), red (fire/water conflict)

Entryway/Foyer

The entry is where chi enters your home. Colors here set the tone for the entire house.

Best Colors:

  • Yellow - Welcoming and uplifting
  • Earth tones - Grounding and stable
  • Green - New beginnings and growth
  • Based on facing direction (see chart below)

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best feng shui color for wealth?

Purple and green are the top wealth colors. Purple represents prosperity in Chinese culture, while green (wood element) represents growth and new money coming in. Gold accents also attract wealth energy. See our Wealth Corner Guide for placement tips.

Can I paint my front door red?

Red front door is auspicious in many cases, but depends on your door's facing direction. South-facing doors benefit from red (fire element). East/Southeast doors benefit from green. Check your door's compass direction before painting. Learn more in our Entryway Feng Shui Guide.

What colors should I avoid in the bedroom?

Avoid bright red (too stimulating for sleep), blue (too cold for romance), and too much green (growth energy disrupts rest). Also avoid black as the dominant color. Stick to skin tones, soft pinks, and earth tones for best results.

How do I find my personal lucky colors?
