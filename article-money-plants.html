3. Lucky Bamboo

If you are busy and forget to water plants, this is for you. It grows in water and represents flexibility.

The Money Tree (Pachira aquatica) is the #1 choice! Its braided trunk represents locked-in fortune, and five-lobed leaves attract wealth energy. Place in Southeast (wealth corner) or on your desk. Jade Plant is second - its round leaves resemble coins and symbolize prosperity.

Best locations: 1) Wealth corner (far left from entrance), 2) Southeast corner of any room, 3) Office desk (far left corner), 4) Living room (near entrance to welcome wealth). Avoid: Bathroom (drains wealth), bedroom (too yang), or directly behind you while working.

A dying plant is worse than no plant - it symbolizes declining fortune. Some believe plants absorb negative energy and "sacrifice" themselves. If yours is dying: check watering/light needs, remove it immediately if dead, thank it for its service, and replace with a healthy plant. Also examine what stressors might be in your life.

Real plants are always preferred because they contain living "chi" energy. However, high-quality silk plants are acceptable if you truly cannot maintain live plants. They must be kept dust-free and look realistic. Avoid cheap plastic plants - they attract stagnant energy rather than prosperity.

Quality over quantity! One thriving, healthy plant is better than multiple struggling ones. If adding more, use lucky numbers: 3 (growth), 6 (prosperity), or 8 (wealth). Avoid 4 plants (unlucky in Chinese). Place in different sectors - wealth corner, office, living room - rather than clustering together.
