üß≠ Two Methods: Compass vs. BTB

There are two primary schools of thought on how to apply the Bagua to your home. Understanding both helps you choose the right approach for your situation.

üß≠ Compass School (Form School)

The classical, traditional Chinese method used by master practitioners for thousands of years.

  • Uses actual compass directions (North, South, East, West)
  • North = Career, South = Fame, regardless of door placement
  • More precise and widely used in Asia
  • Requires a compass reading from center of home
  • Best for: Houses, fixed structures, precise work

üö™ BTB / Western School

Developed by Professor Lin Yun, popular in Western countries since the 1980s.

  • Aligns bottom of Bagua with front door wall
  • No compass needed ‚Äî based on entry orientation
  • Simpler for beginners to learn and apply
  • Works well for apartments and rentals
  • Best for: Beginners, apartments, quick assessments

üí° Which Method Should You Choose?

I recommend the Compass School for anyone serious about feng shui. It's more accurate and has 3,000+ years of proven results. However, if you live in an apartment building where the compass reading might be affected by metal structures, or if you're just starting out, the BTB method is a perfectly valid starting point. The most important thing is to pick one method and stick with it ‚Äî don't mix them.

üìê Step-by-Step: How to Map Your Home

Mapping Your Home with the Compass Method

1
Get Your Floor Plan

Draw or print a simple floor plan of your home. Include all rooms, doors, and windows. It doesn't need to be architecturally perfect ‚Äî a rough sketch works.

2
Find the Center

Mark the geometric center of your floor plan. For regular shapes (squares, rectangles), this is where diagonals cross. For L-shapes, calculate as if the missing section exists, then mark the center of the complete rectangle.

3
Take a Compass Reading

Stand at the center of your home with a compass (phone compass works). Identify North. Important: Stand away from electronics and metal objects for accuracy. Take 3 readings and average them.

4
Divide into 9 Sectors

Draw a 3√ó3 grid over your floor plan, creating 9 equal sections. Align the grid so North on your compass matches the North section of the grid (Career sector).

5
Label Each Sector

Mark each section with its Bagua name: SE=Wealth, S=Fame, SW=Love, E=Family, Center=Health, W=Children, NE=Knowledge, N=Career, NW=Helpful People.

6
Identify Issues & Prioritize

Note which rooms fall in which sectors. Is your bathroom in the Wealth corner? Is the bedroom in the Career sector? Identify problems and prioritize the life area you most want to improve.

üî≥ Dealing with Missing Corners

Modern homes are rarely perfect rectangles. L-shaped, U-shaped, and irregularly shaped floor plans create "missing corners" ‚Äî sectors of the Bagua that don't exist in your physical space. These missing sectors can create significant challenges in the corresponding life areas.

‚ö†Ô∏è How to Identify Missing Corners

Draw your floor plan as a rectangle by extending lines to include the largest possible shape. Any area outside your actual walls but inside this rectangle is a "missing corner." If the missing portion is more than 1/3 of the total sector area, it counts as significantly missing.

üõ†Ô∏è Remedies for Missing Corners:

  • Outdoor cure: Place a light, flag, tall plant, or garden feature in the missing area outside to "extend" the energy
  • Mirror cure: Hang a mirror on the inside wall adjacent to the missing section ‚Äî it visually extends the space
  • Symbolic cure: Place objects representing that life area in the nearest adjacent corner (e.g., love symbols in the room closest to the missing SW corner)
  • Element cure: Strengthen the corresponding element in rooms that border the missing section
  • Crystal cure: Place a faceted crystal ball at the junction point where the "missing" begins to redirect chi

üè¢ Applying Bagua to Apartments & Single Rooms

Apartments

For apartments, apply the Bagua to your individual unit only ‚Äî not the entire building. Use your apartment's front door as the reference point. The compass method still works perfectly, just take your reading inside your unit.

Individual Rooms

You can apply a "mini Bagua" to any room. Stand at the room's main doorway looking in. The wall containing the door is always the bottom of the Bagua (Knowledge-Career-Helpful People). This creates a room-level Bagua that adds an extra layer of refinement to your feng shui.

This is why feng shui practitioners often place specific items in the far-left corner of your desk or bedroom ‚Äî it activates that room's mini wealth sector.

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Bagua map in feng shui?
The Bagua (ÂÖ´Âç¶) map is an octagonal grid containing 9 sectors, each representing a different life area: Wealth, Fame, Love, Family, Health, Children, Knowledge, Career, and Helpful People. By overlaying the Bagua onto your home's floor plan, you can identify which physical areas correspond to which life aspects and activate them accordingly.
How do I overlay the Bagua map on my home?
There are two main methods: 1) Compass School ‚Äî Use an actual compass to determine true North, then align the Bagua with cardinal directions. 2) BTB/Western School ‚Äî Align the bottom of the Bagua with your front door wall. The Compass method is more precise and widely used in classical feng shui.
What if a Bagua sector is missing from my home?
Irregular-shaped homes often have "missing corners." To remedy, place a mirror on the adjacent wall to visually extend the space, install outdoor features like lights or plants in the missing area, or place symbols of that life area in the nearest accessible corner.
Can I use the Bagua for individual rooms?
Yes! Apply a "mini Bagua" to each room by aligning it with the room's main entry door. The far-left corner from the door is the room's wealth corner, and the far-right is the love corner. This is especially useful for apartments or detailed room design.
